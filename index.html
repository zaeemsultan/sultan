Spinal Conditions

Conditions We Treat

Dr. Sultan specialises in the assessment, diagnosis, and surgical management of a comprehensive range of spinal conditions. Each patient is assessed individually, with imaging reviewed in detail prior to any management recommendation.

Disc Herniation

Cervical, thoracic, and lumbar disc prolapse causing nerve compression, radiculopathy, or myelopathy. Treatment ranges from injection therapy to microsurgical discectomy.

Spinal Stenosis

Narrowing of the spinal canal or neuroforamina causing claudication, leg weakness, or loss of balance. Lumbar and cervical stenosis managed with decompression surgery when indicated.

Spondylolisthesis

Forward slippage of a vertebra causing instability, pain, and neurological symptoms. Management includes conservative options through to fusion surgery depending on grade and symptoms.

Degenerative Spine Disorders

Age-related changes including degenerative disc disease, facet arthropathy, and multilevel degenerative disease. Both cervical spondylotic myelopathy and lumbar degenerative conditions treated.

Spinal Tumours

Primary and metastatic tumours of the spine including intradural, extradural, and vertebral body tumours. Oncological liaison and multidisciplinary planning integral to management.

Spinal Infections

Pyogenic and tuberculous vertebral osteomyelitis and epidural abscess. Experience with complex presentations including subdural extension, drawing on published case review experience.

Failed Back Surgery Syndrome

Assessment and management of persistent or recurrent symptoms following previous spine surgery. Comprehensive revision surgery planning with detailed pre-operative workup.

Spinal Trauma

Thoracolumbar fractures, cervical spine injuries, and traumatic instability. Management informed by AO Spine classification and evidence-based protocols developed during NHS training.

Cervical Myelopathy

Spinal cord dysfunction secondary to cervical stenosis, ossification, or instability. Surgical decompression — anterior or posterior — tailored to imaging and neurological status.

Surgical Expertise

Spine Procedures

Each surgical plan is formulated after thorough clinical assessment, imaging review, and discussion with the patient. The aim is always the least invasive procedure that achieves the clinical goal.

01

Minimally Invasive Spine Surgery (MIS)

Tubular retractor discectomy, MIS decompression, and percutaneous pedicle screw instrumentation. MIS approaches minimise soft tissue disruption, reduce blood loss, shorten hospital stay, and accelerate recovery.

02

Microsurgical Lumbar Discectomy

Gold-standard microsurgical technique for lumbar disc herniation, performed under magnification to achieve precise nerve root decompression with minimal tissue injury.

03

Anterior Cervical Discectomy & Fusion (ACDF)

Treatment for cervical disc disease, radiculopathy, and myelopathy through an anterior approach. ACDF reliably decompresses the spinal cord and nerve roots while stabilising the motion segment.

04

Lumbar Decompression & Fusion

TLIF, PLIF, and ALIF approaches for spinal instability, spondylolisthesis, and degenerative disc disease. Instrumented fusion restores alignment and eliminates painful motion.

05

Spinal Tumour Surgery

Resection or debulking of intradural, extradural, and intramedullary spinal tumours. Integrated with oncological planning and, where appropriate, intraoperative neuromonitoring.

06

Posterior Cervical Decompression

Laminectomy, laminoplasty, and foraminotomy for multilevel cervical stenosis and cervical myelopathy where a posterior approach is clinically optimal.

07

Revision Spine Surgery

Complex re-operative surgery for adjacent segment disease, failed fusion, pseudarthrosis, and recurrent disc herniation. Detailed pre-operative planning is essential in revision cases.

08

Vertebral Augmentation

Vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty for osteoporotic vertebral compression fractures, providing immediate pain relief and structural support.

09

Laminectomy & Laminotomy

Targeted bony decompression for spinal stenosis, ligamentum flavum hypertrophy, and compressive pathology without destabilising the spine.

10

Micro-endoscopic Procedures

Endoscopic and tubular approaches to spine pathology, including unilateral laminotomy for bilateral decompression (ULBD) — among the least invasive methods for spinal canal decompression.

International Service

Spine Second Opinion

If you have been advised to undergo spinal surgery and wish to understand your options more fully, or if your diagnosis is uncertain, Dr. Sultan provides a rigorous, internationally recognised spine second opinion service.

Why Seek a Second Opinion?

  • You have been recommended surgery and want to explore all options
  • You have had a previous spine operation with unsatisfactory results
  • Your symptoms do not correlate with the proposed diagnosis
  • You wish to understand whether minimally invasive alternatives exist
  • You require an independent assessment before committing to a treatment plan
  • Your treating surgeon has recommended a complex multi-level procedure

The Second Opinion Process

📋

Case Submission

Submit a brief clinical summary: your main symptoms, onset, duration, any previous spine surgery, and current medications. This can be provided via secure WhatsApp message or email.

🧬

Imaging Upload

Send your spinal MRI and/or CT images. DICOM files are preferred. High-resolution scanned images or a secure cloud link (WeTransfer, Dropbox, Google Drive) are also accepted. Include all relevant radiology reports.

🔍

Expert Review

Dr. Sultan conducts a thorough personal review of all submitted materials, correlating clinical information with imaging findings and applying current evidence-based guidelines.

📝

Written Report

A detailed, structured written report is produced, covering the diagnosis, agreement or disagreement with the proposed plan, alternative treatment options, and a clear recommendation.

📅

Timeline

Standard second opinion reports are delivered within 5–7 working days of receipt of all materials. An expedited service may be available for urgent cases — please enquire at the time of submission.

Confidentiality & Data Protection

All patient information submitted for second opinion review is treated with the strictest professional confidentiality. Data is used solely for the purpose of providing a clinical opinion and is not shared with any third party without express patient consent. Digital communications are conducted via encrypted or password-protected channels wherever possible. This service does not create a formal doctor-patient relationship equivalent to a full clinical consultation and does not replace the advice of your primary treating clinician.
